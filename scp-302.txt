**Item #:** SCP-302

**Object Class**: Safe

**Special Containment Procedures:** SCP-302 is to kept in site [REDACTED], under Safe-3 Protocols. Artifact should be handled with gloves at all times, and utmost precautions should be taken to ensure that the artfifact does not make skin contact with unauthorized personnel. Any personnel not scheduled for testing that start showing signs of SCP-302's effects may apply for termination. All subjects suffering from SCP-302's affliction should be terminated after no longer than nine (9) days.

**Description:** SCP-302 is a small tin sculpture with a bronze/patina finish depicting two ants carrying a leaf. Whenever a human makes direct skin contact with the artifact, they will invariably find a single small, relatively harmless ant on their person within (2) hours. This ant may of any several very small ant species, and rarely causes much alarm. It is of note that in all recorded cases, subjects have always distinctly noticed this first ant and fully remember seeing it.

Thereafter, an exponentially increasing numbers of ants will appear on the exposed subject by day. Throughout the entire day, the number of ants appears seem to be in the range of 3^^X^^ to 5^^X^^, where X is the number of days since initial contact with SCP-302. 

As time progresses, not only does the number of ants increase, but the species of ants seem to change, with smaller, harmless species appearing at first, and later changing to larger species or species with more painful stings or bites.


**Addendum 302-01**: Subject of Test Log 2 was a single D-Class Personnel. Subject was told he was in a "Study testing how a week of relaxation might affect the performance of D-Class Personnel", and kept in a "relaxation chamber" with exercise equipment, various books and magazines, and a color television. Subject was unknowingly exposed to SCP-302.

**<<Test Log 2>>**
> **Days 1 & 2**
> Subject spends his time in leisurely pursuits. Subject reports greats satisfaction, and nothing out of the ordinary.
> **Day 3**
> Subject reports small ant problem, and admits it's probably his fault for letting crumbs fall over the couch and carpet. Had noticed an ant two days ago, but didn't think it was worth mentioning. Subject reported great satisfaction otherwise, and requested a can of bug spray.
> **Day 4** 
> Subject reports that ant problem persists besides his best efforts to spray room, and that ants have become more numerous and occasionally painful. Subject reports that the ants seem bigger and different from those he saw yesterday. Subject still reports relative happiness with the experiment.
> **Day 5**
> Subject reports great annoyance and increased pain, as ants are nearly always on his person now. Ants seem different once more, and more aggressive and agitated. Subject clearly suspects ants are not of a normal nature, at this point.
> **Day 6**
> Subject in great pain. On average, five (5) tropical green-head ants are seen to appear every minute on subject throughout entire day. Subject became aggressive due to pain, and demanded to be released from chamber. Pretense was dropped, and subject was restrained and given medical attention.
> **Day 7**
> Subject in great pain. An average of twenty-five (25) tropical fire ants are seen to appear every minute on subject. Subject requires continuous ant removal.
> **Day 8**
> Subject is heavily medicated, and unconscious through majority of day. An average of 140 bullet ants appear on subject each minute. Rapid ant-removal is necessary and anti-inflammatory medicine is necessary.
> **Day 9**
> [DATA EXPUNGED]. Subject and experiment are terminated.
