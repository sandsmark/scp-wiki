Hey all, I'm [[*user barredowl]], a somewhat ambitious artist. I have yet to buy any quality drawing equipment and mostly draw via Microsoft's Paint 3D/Photopea. However, it does its job, and maybe sometime in the future I'll upload some art I drew on paper.

++ The Process

So usually, when I'm thinking of drawing an SCP, I usually tend towards a situation which I can easily display. For example, maybe the interview log in [[[SCP-3980]]], or the gramophone from [[[SCP-3939]]]. If it's just a simple humanoid, I usually just draw their face. I occasionally just draw a character from the SCP, such as the Masquerade figure from [[[SCP-4430]]].

After I've fully convinced myself that drawing that part of the SCP is going to be fun, I head over to a [https://lospec.com/palette-list palette list] and select something that catches my eye (usually a four-color palette, since that's the easiest to handle in Paint 3D). I download it via a .txt file and head over to Paint 3D to select the individual colors using a hex code.

Then, I shift the canvas to an appropriate size. If it's smaller (i.e, a humanoid picture) I usually choose a 30x30 canvas. If it's larger (i.e a situation I want to illustrate) I shoot for a 60x60. Then, I get to drawing, expand the canvas to 300x300 once it's finished, and publish it here!
